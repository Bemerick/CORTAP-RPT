## Deficiency Summary

**Total Deficiencies:** 8 (1 Procurement, 5 DBE, 2 PTASP)

**Repeat Deficiencies:** 1 (DBE5-1 - repeat from FY 2016 and FY 2019 Triennial Reviews)

**Deficiency Details:**

### Deficiency 1: P12-4
- **Review Area:** Procurement
- **Code:** P12-4
- **Description:** Contract files lacking signed Buy America certifications. The procurement of Cummins Diesel Engines procured on 5/16/2022 did not have evidence that a signed Buy America Certification was received. 49 CFR 661.6 requires contractors that apply or bid for an award exceeding $150,000 to file the required Buy America certification.
- **Corrective Action:** By October 2, 2023, MEVA must submit documentation that the Cummins Diesel Engines procured on 5/16/2022 complies with Buy America provisions. MEVA updated Procurement Policies and Procedures and provided documentation of implementation in more recent procurement.
- **Due Date:** October 2, 2023
- **Status:** Open

### Deficiency 2: DBE5-1* (REPEAT from FY 2016 and FY 2019)
- **Review Area:** Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE)
- **Code:** DBE5-1
- **Description:** DBE uniform reports contain inaccuracies and/or are missing required information. Semi-annual reports for December 2021 and June 2022 had discrepancies: (1) contract for $1,169,987 in December 2021 report not found on procurement list; (2) construction contract awarded 1/21/2022 for $2,482,387 not included in June 2022 report; (3) service contract awarded August 2022 for $1,045,387 not in December 2022 report; (4) semi-annual reports June 2019-December 2022 incorrectly included payments to subcontractors as new awards. Staff member who completed reports no longer works at MEVA. Reports requiring corrections: FY 2019 through FY 2022.
- **Corrective Action:** By November 30, 2023, MEVA must submit corrected FY 2019-2022 Uniform Reports in TrAMS, notify FTA TCR, and submit revised DBE Program plan describing procedures for correctly completing reports. Technical assistance provided to new DBELO.
- **Due Date:** November 30, 2023
- **Status:** Open

### Deficiency 3: DBE8-1
- **Review Area:** Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE)
- **Code:** DBE8-1
- **Description:** Inadequate implementation of race-neutral measures. Per 49 CFR 26.51(a), recipient must meet maximum feasible portion of overall goal using race-neutral means. DBE Program plan and FY 2022-2025 DBE Goal and Methodology do not describe race-neutral measures. MEVA could not provide list of race-neutral measures or documentation of implementation.
- **Corrective Action:** By November 30, 2023, MEVA must submit implementation plan (including procurement coordination) for race-neutral measures with evidence of implementation, and revised DBE Program plan describing race-neutral measures.
- **Due Date:** November 30, 2023
- **Status:** Open

### Deficiency 4: DBE8-2
- **Review Area:** Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE)
- **Code:** DBE8-2
- **Description:** Small business element not implemented. Per 49 CFR 26.39(c), recipient must actively implement DBE program elements to foster small business participation. MEVA's DBE Program does not describe small business measures. Documentation included transportation provider vendor list and website snapshot but inadequate evidence of fostering small business participation.
- **Corrective Action:** By November 30, 2023, MEVA must submit evidence of implementing small business participation strategies (including procurement coordination) and revised DBE Program plan describing small business elements.
- **Due Date:** November 30, 2023
- **Status:** Open

### Deficiency 5: DBE9-2
- **Review Area:** Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE)
- **Code:** DBE9-2
- **Description:** Inadequate good faith efforts determination. Per 49 CFR 26.53(b)(c)(d), when procuring with established DBE contract goal, award must go to bidder/offeror making good faith efforts. Riverbank Restoration contract solicitation had 5% DBE goal but incorrectly cited 49 CFR 23 (correct regulation is 49 CFR 26). MEVA did not provide documentation verifying bidder met DBE goal with certified DBEs or that listed DBEs had appropriate NAICS codes for work to be performed.
- **Corrective Action:** By November 30, 2023, MEVA must submit method for determining good faith efforts and/or evidence of documentation in procurement files, and revised DBE Program plan describing good faith efforts methods.
- **Due Date:** November 30, 2023
- **Status:** Open

### Deficiency 6: DBE12-5
- **Review Area:** Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE)
- **Code:** DBE12-5
- **Description:** Recipient does not implement DBE termination/substitution provisions. Per 49 CFR 26.53(f), contractor may not terminate DBE without prior written consent of recipient. DBE Program plan, Procurement and Purchasing Manual, and Riverbank Restoration contract do not describe in detail the procedural requirements MEVA and contractor must take prior to terminating/substituting a DBE. Must include 5-day notice to DBE, DBE response period, and good cause determination.
- **Corrective Action:** By November 30, 2023, MEVA must submit procedures for ensuring good cause and due process for DBE termination/substitution, and revised DBE Program plan describing procedural steps.
- **Due Date:** November 30, 2023
- **Status:** Open

### Deficiency 7: PTASP1-5
- **Review Area:** Public Transportation Agency Safety Plans (PTASP)
- **Code:** PTASP1-5
- **Description:** ASP not approved by Safety Committee. Per 49 U.S.C. § 5329(d), transit agency receiving Section 5307 funding serving large urbanized area must establish Safety Committee by July 31, 2022. Once established, Safety Committee should approve/update ASP incorporating PTASP requirements by December 31, 2022. MEVA's PTASP documentation did not include evidence of Safety Committee approval.
- **Corrective Action:** By October 6, 2023, MEVA must submit evidence that Safety Committee approved its Agency Safety Plan.
- **Due Date:** October 6, 2023
- **Status:** Open

### Deficiency 8: PTASP2-6
- **Review Area:** Public Transportation Agency Safety Plans (PTASP)
- **Code:** PTASP2-6
- **Description:** Safety Committee is not responsible for minimum requirements. Per 49 U.S.C. 5329(d)(5), Safety Committee must: (1) identify and recommend risk-based mitigations to reduce likelihood and severity of consequences identified through safety risk assessment; (2) identify mitigations/strategies that may be ineffective, inappropriate, or not implemented as intended; (3) identify safety deficiencies for continuous improvement. Information provided did not document these required duties.
- **Corrective Action:** By October 6, 2023, MEVA must submit evidence that Safety Committee is responsible for minimum requirements.
- **Due Date:** October 6, 2023
- **Status:** Open

## Recipient Description (Background Info)

- **Organization Type:** Regional transit authority established in 1974 under Chapter 161B of Massachusetts General Laws
- **Governance:** Advisory board with one member from each of 16 served communities; board hires administrator, sets fares, establishes service levels, authorizes real estate purchases
- **Service Area:** 16 communities in northeast Massachusetts
  - **Cities:** Lawrence, Methuen, Haverhill, Amesbury, Newburyport
  - **Towns:** Andover, Boxford, Groveland, Georgetown, Merrimac, Newbury, North Andover, North Reading, Rowley, Salisbury, West Newbury
- **Service Area Population:** 384,375 (within Boston Urbanized Area)
- **Service Area Size:** 284 square miles
- **Fleet Size:** 60 buses for fixed-route service, 35 vans for ADA paratransit (all FTA-funded)
- **Routes:** 25 fixed routes plus demand-response services
- **Service Hours:** M-F 5:00 AM - 8:00 PM, Sat 7:00 AM - 7:30 PM, ADA paratransit same hours
- **Facilities (5 FTA-funded):**
  1. Bus garage and administrative facility (Haverhill)
  2. Transit center (Amesbury)
  3. Multimodal transit center and parking garage (Lawrence)
  4. Surface parking facilities (Lawrence)
  5. Structured parking garage (Haverhill)
