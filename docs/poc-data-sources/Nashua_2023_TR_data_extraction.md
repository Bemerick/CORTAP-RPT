## Deficiency Summary

**Total Deficiencies:** 1 (Financial Management and Capacity only)

**Repeat Deficiencies:** 0 (no repeat deficiencies from FY 2019 Triennial Review)

**Deficiency Details:**

### Deficiency 1: F6-3
- **Review Area:** Financial Management and Capacity
- **Code:** F6-3
- **Description:** Single Audit submission deficient, including late submission. Per 2 CFR Part 200.512 (Uniform Guidance), recipients that meet the threshold for a Single Audit must complete and submit to the Federal Audit Clearinghouse (FAC) its Single Audit and the data collection form within 30 calendar days after receipt of the auditor's report or nine months after the end of the audit period, whichever is earlier. The City of Nashua's fiscal year ends June 30th, so it is required to submit its Single Audit report and data collection form by March 30th. Due to staffing shortages during the review period:
  - FY2020 Single Audit and data collection form were submitted nearly three months late into the FAC
  - FY2021 Single Audit and data collection form were submitted 13 months late
  - FY2022 Single Audit and data collection form were due March 30, 2023, and have not yet been submitted (as of review date)
- **Corrective Action:** By March 30, 2024, NTS must upload into OTrak procedures to ensure that the Single Audit report, data collection form, and reporting package are submitted on time in the Federal Audit Clearinghouse. NTS shall notify the FTA regional office via email once the procedures have been uploaded into OTrak.
- **Due Date:** March 30, 2024
- **Status:** Open (no closure date shown in report)

## Recipient Description (Background Info)

- **Organization Type:** City department (Nashua Transit System established in 1984)
- **Department Structure:** NTS is department within City of Nashua's Community Development Division
- **Governance:** Transit Administrator oversees day-to-day operations and reports to Community Development Division Director. Division Director functions as NTS' Chief Executive Officer, reports to Mayor, responsible for broad oversight of NTS and five other City departments.
- **Service Area:** 9 communities in New Hampshire
  - **Primary:** Nashua
  - **Also served:** Amherst, Brookline, Hollis, Hudson, Merrimack, Milford, Mont Vernon, Wilton
- **Service Area Population:** More than 200,000 people served; Nashua urbanized area approximately 236,000
- **Fleet Size:** 21 FTA-funded buses for fixed-route service, 10 federally funded paratransit vans
- **Service Hours:**
  - **Weekdays:** 6:00 AM - 10:45 PM
  - **Saturdays:** 9:00 AM - 10:45 PM
  - **Sundays:** No service
  - **ADA Paratransit:** Same hours/days as fixed-route
- **Fares:**

  - **Honored Citizens (disabled/Medicare/veterans):** $0.60
  - **Seniors (60+) and children (5 and under):** Free
